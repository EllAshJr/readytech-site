# Instant Estimate Review & Verification Call

**Audience:** Sales technicians
**Read time:** 7 minutes
**Use this when:** A prospect submits the website Instant Estimate.

> **Sales outcome:** Verify the inputs, uncover business impact, and convert the estimate into the paid Uptime Assessment or approved next step.

## Before calling

Review:

- Business type and location count
- Selected services
- Device/user/storage counts
- Setup and monthly estimate
- Complexity level and warnings
- Missing or contradictory answers
- Service-zone implications

## Verification call agenda (10–15 minutes)

1. Confirm the business problem in the customer’s words.
2. Confirm which systems stop when the problem occurs.
3. Verify locations, users, devices, equipment, and timeline.
4. Correct assumptions in the estimate.
5. Explain whether onsite assessment or engineering review is required.
6. Schedule the next step before ending the call.

## High-value questions

- “What happens to revenue or staff when this system is unavailable?”
- “Who else needs to approve the work?”
- “What date or event is driving the timeline?”
- “Which vendor owns the POS, carrier, cabling, or hardware today?”
- “Has anyone documented the current network?”

## Estimate language

> “This is a starting estimate based on the information entered. It becomes a final proposal only after ReadyTech verifies the environment, assumptions, and third-party requirements.”

## Conversion decision

| Situation | Next step |
|---|---|
| Standard, well-documented, low risk | Confirm scope and proposal process |
| Unknown equipment/cabling/site | Book $349 Uptime Assessment |
| Complex/multi-site/high risk | Assessment + engineering review |
| Customer not ready | Set dated follow-up and status |
| Outside model | Refer or close politely |

## Call close

> “The most direct next step is to reserve the Uptime Assessment. That gives you verified findings and three options instead of relying on assumptions. I can schedule that now.”

Record all corrections in the quote record; do not rely on memory or email alone.
