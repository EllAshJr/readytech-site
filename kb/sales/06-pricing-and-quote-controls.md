# Pricing, Deposits, Exclusions & Quote Controls

**Audience:** Sales technicians
**Read time:** 6 minutes
**Use this when:** Preparing estimates, discussing price, or deciding whether engineering approval is required.

> **Sales outcome:** Quote consistently, protect margin, and avoid promising work that has not been verified.

## Public starting points

| Item | Starting policy |
|---|---:|
| Uptime Assessment | $349 |

## Payment controls

- Collect **50% of project labor** before scheduling.
- Collect **100% of hardware cost** before ordering.
- Remaining balance follows the signed scope.
- Managed-service customers should use autopay.
- Standard managed-service term: **12 months**, unless approved otherwise.

## Mandatory pricing statement

> Published prices are starting points. Final scope and pricing follow a ReadyTech Uptime Assessment. Hardware, carriers, cabling, subscriptions, permits, and third-party fees are separate unless included in writing.

## Never hide these cost categories

- Hardware and shipping
- Carrier/data plans
- Cabling, lifts, construction, permits
- Third-party software subscriptions
- Travel or extended-zone trip fees
- After-hours cutover
- Taxes where applicable

## Use assumptions explicitly

Example:

> “Estimate assumes one location, existing usable cabling, business-hours access, supported firewall, and no construction work.”

If an assumption changes, the price may change.

## When a sales technician may quote directly

- The product/tier is standard
- Inputs are complete
- Device count is within published limits
- No unsupported equipment is known
- No major cabling or construction is expected
- No regulated or high-risk environment is involved

## When to stop and escalate

- Multi-site routing or complex VPN
- More than 20–30 monitored devices
- Unknown/obsolete equipment
- High-density or outdoor Wi-Fi
- Overnight POS cutover
- Complex carrier design
- Compliance commitments
- Custom integrations
- Customer demands a guarantee or penalty clause

Do not discount recurring service without approval. Credit the assessment rather than weakening the monthly plan.
