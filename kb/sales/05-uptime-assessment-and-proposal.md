# Uptime Assessment & Good/Better/Best Proposal

**Audience:** Sales technicians
**Read time:** 6 minutes
**Use this when:** Selling the assessment, setting expectations, or preparing the proposal handoff.

> **Sales outcome:** Turn an uncertain technical problem into a paid discovery engagement and a clear three-option decision.

## What the $349 assessment delivers

- Current-state summary
- Internet/firewall and Wi-Fi review
- POS or business-device review when relevant
- Backup-internet readiness
- Risk findings and business impact
- Top three recommended improvements
- Good / Better / Best proposal

The fee may be credited toward projects above $2,000 approved within 30 days. Do not describe the assessment as a free sales visit.

## How to explain it

> “The estimate gives a starting range. The assessment verifies the environment so the final proposal is accurate and avoids surprises involving cabling, equipment, carriers, access, or vendor limitations.”

## Proposal structure

| Option | Purpose | Typical content |
|---|---|---|
| **Good** | Fix the immediate problem | Repair or limited project |
| **Better** | Fix and monitor | Project + ReadyUptime Managed |
| **Best** | Fix, monitor, and protect continuity | Project + ReadyContinuity Pro + failover/add-on |

The middle option should usually be the sensible operating recommendation. The Best option protects against larger business impact.

## One-page proposal format

1. **Problem:** Customer language and business impact
2. **Findings:** What was verified
3. **Recommendation:** Good/Better/Best
4. **Investment:** Setup, hardware, recurring, exclusions
5. **Expected outcome:** What changes for the business
6. **Next step:** Accept, deposit, and schedule

## Assessment boundaries

The assessment is not destructive troubleshooting and does not promise resolution during the visit. Any change that could interrupt service requires authorization and may become separately billable project work.

## Sales technician checklist

Before proposal preparation, confirm:

- Decision-maker and stakeholders
- Location count and service zone
- Device inventory or assumptions
- Cabling and hardware unknowns
- Carrier and third-party dependencies
- Business-hours versus after-hours implementation
- Required completion date
- Recommended recurring plan

## Close language

> “Which option best matches the level of interruption your business can tolerate?”

This keeps the decision focused on business risk rather than only the lowest price.
