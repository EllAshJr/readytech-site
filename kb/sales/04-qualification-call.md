# 15-Minute Qualification Call

**Audience:** Sales technicians
**Read time:** 6 minutes
**Use this when:** Handling a new lead, Instant Estimate follow-up, or referral introduction.

> **Sales outcome:** Decide whether to book the paid assessment, request more information, escalate, or decline.

## Call structure

| Time | Purpose |
|---:|---|
| 0–2 min | Set the agenda and confirm time |
| 2–7 min | Understand the business problem and impact |
| 7–11 min | Confirm environment, locations, urgency, and authority |
| 11–14 min | Explain the likely next step |
| 14–15 min | Schedule and confirm ownership |

## Opening script

> “I’ll ask a few questions about what is failing, how it affects the business, and what equipment or vendors are involved. Then I’ll tell you whether an Uptime Assessment is the right next step. This should take about 15 minutes.”

## Must-ask questions

1. What problem caused you to contact ReadyTech?
2. What stops or becomes difficult when it happens?
3. How often does it occur, and when did it last happen?
4. How many locations are affected?
5. Which systems depend on the network—POS, payments, Wi-Fi, phones, cloud apps, cameras, printers?
6. Who currently supports the environment?
7. Are you the person approving the work? Who else participates?
8. Is this urgent, planned, or tied to an opening/changeover date?
9. Is onsite work likely required?
10. Is there a budget range or approved project?

## Listen for business impact

Translate technical symptoms into cost or risk:

- Lost payments or orders
- Staff unable to work
- Customers unable to connect
- Remote access unavailable
- Vendor blame and delays
- Repeated truck rolls or after-hours emergencies

## Qualification outcomes

| Outcome | Action |
|---|---|
| Strong fit, unknown environment | Book $349 Uptime Assessment |
| Clear small scope, enough detail | Prepare estimate, still verify before final proposal |
| Complex/multi-site/high risk | Book assessment and engineering review |
| No authority/budget/timeline | Nurture; do not over-scope |
| Outside service model | Refer or decline professionally |

## Close the call

> “Based on what you described, the next step is the ReadyTech Uptime Assessment. We will document the environment, identify the main risks, and give you Good, Better, and Best options. The assessment is $349 and is credited toward approved projects over $2,000 within 30 days.”

Document the problem in the customer’s words before ending the call.
