# Proposal, Close, Payment & Handoff

**Audience:** Sales technicians
**Read time:** 7 minutes
**Use this when:** The customer is choosing an option or has approved the proposal.

> **Sales outcome:** Convert approval into signed scope, payment, scheduled implementation, and a complete technical handoff.

## Closing sequence

```text
Confirm problem → Present three options → Ask for choice
→ Resolve scope questions → Obtain signature → Collect payment
→ Schedule → Handoff to delivery
```

## Present the recommendation

Start with the business problem and risk, then present Good/Better/Best. State which option ReadyTech recommends and why.

Example:

> “Because payment and POS availability directly affect revenue, ReadyTech recommends ReadyContinuity Pro rather than monitoring alone. It adds managed failover testing and priority response.”

## Ask directly

> “Would you like to move forward with the Better option, or do you want the additional continuity protection in Best?”

Do not finish a proposal call with “think about it and let me know” without agreeing on a specific next step and date.

## Required before scheduling

- Signed scope of work
- 50% labor deposit
- 100% hardware payment
- Managed-service agreement if applicable
- Autopay authorization if applicable
- Approved implementation/cutover window
- Site access and authorized contacts

## Sales-to-delivery handoff packet

Include:

- Customer contacts and decision-maker
- Approved option and signed scope
- Business problem and success criteria
- Locations and service zone
- Inventory, diagrams, photos, and vendor contacts
- Hardware ordered
- Carrier/cabling dependencies
- Access restrictions and hours
- Assumptions/exclusions
- Deadline and promised response targets
- Open risks or engineering notes

## Promise control

Only promise what appears in the signed scope. Never promise:

- Guaranteed resolution time
- Unsupported third-party behavior
- Unapproved after-hours work
- “Unlimited” support
- A staffed 24/7 NOC

## Post-install path

Installation acceptance → managed-service onboarding → 30-day review → quarterly review → referral request.
