# Ideal Customer, Buying Triggers & Territory

**Audience:** Sales technicians
**Read time:** 5 minutes
**Use this when:** Deciding whether a lead deserves a call, assessment, or polite decline.

> **Sales outcome:** Spend time on businesses with real uptime pain, authority to buy, and a path to recurring service.

## Primary ideal customer

An independent restaurant, fast-casual operator, retailer, or local service business with:

| Attribute | Best fit |
|---|---|
| Locations | 1–5 |
| Employees | 10–75 |
| Internal IT | None or very limited |
| Technology | Cloud POS, Wi-Fi, online orders, cameras, printers, cloud apps |
| Business risk | Revenue or operations stop when connectivity fails |
| Buying trigger | New site, recurring outage, POS change, expansion, weak Wi-Fi |

## Secondary customers

Professional offices, law/accounting/real-estate firms, salons, construction or field offices, churches, nonprofits, and small warehouse/logistics operations can be strong fits once the core process is repeatable.

## Strong buying signals

- “Our POS or payments go down.”
- “Wi-Fi is unreliable in parts of the building.”
- “We have several vendors and no one owns the problem.”
- “We are opening or acquiring another location.”
- “The owner needs remote access.”
- “We cannot afford internet downtime.”
- “We do not know what equipment we have.”

## Low-fit or high-risk leads

Escalate or decline when the prospect wants:

- Residential break/fix as the main engagement
- Enterprise-scale 24/7 support without enterprise budget
- Complex healthcare compliance with no approved specialist
- Custom software development
- Self-hosted production cloud infrastructure
- The lowest hourly rate with no interest in managed service
- A one-time emergency repair with no access, documentation, or authorization

## Geographic model

### Austin–Manor: core onsite zone

Routine assessments, installations, normal onsite pricing, and faster field response.

### Houston: extended service zone

Remote monitoring/support, scheduled project days, travel charges when applicable, and coordinated local field partners when needed.

Never promise identical onsite response across all three cities.

## Quick lead score

Give one point for each “yes”:

1. Is the problem tied to revenue or operations?
2. Is there a decision-maker on the call?
3. Is the business within the service model?
4. Is there budget for an assessment or managed plan?
5. Is there a recurring-service opportunity?

**4–5:** strong lead. **2–3:** qualify carefully. **0–1:** do not overinvest.
