# Estimate Acceptance, Rejection & Follow-Up

**Audience:** Sales technicians
**Read time:** 6 minutes
**Use this when:** An estimate is accepted, declined, requests a call, or receives no response.

> **Sales outcome:** Every quote has a documented status, next action, owner, and date.

## Status workflow

```text
Sent → Viewed → Verification Call → Assessment/Proposal
     ↘ Accepted Pending Verification
     ↘ Rejected
     ↘ No Response / Nurture
```

## Accepted estimate

Acceptance means the customer wants to proceed to verification—not that unverified work is automatically scheduled.

Actions:

1. Contact within the published business-hours target.
2. Confirm scope, decision-maker, and timeline.
3. Book assessment/verification.
4. Prepare final proposal and agreement.
5. Collect deposit/hardware payment before scheduling.

## Rejected estimate

Ask one neutral question:

> “To help us improve, was the decision mainly timing, scope, price, another provider, or something else?”

Record the reason. Do not argue. If the issue is scope or timing, offer a revised call. If the issue is price, revisit business impact and options—not an immediate discount.

## Request-a-call

Treat as a high-priority warm lead. Review the estimate before calling and use the verification-call KB.

## No-response cadence

- Day 0: Estimate email
- Day 1: Short confirmation/check for questions
- Day 3: Invite verification call
- Day 7: Business-impact reminder and assessment option
- Day 14: Expiration/final follow-up
- Then move to nurture unless there is a live deadline

## Follow-up message

> “I wanted to confirm you received estimate RT-____. The fastest way to make it accurate is a short verification call. We will confirm the environment, explain the options, and identify whether an onsite Uptime Assessment is needed.”

## Required record fields

- Status
- Last contact date
- Next action/date
- Objection/reason
- Decision-maker
- Estimated setup and monthly value
- Assigned owner

No estimate should remain in “sent” indefinitely.
