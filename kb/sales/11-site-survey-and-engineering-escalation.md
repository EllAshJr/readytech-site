# Site Survey & Engineering Escalation Rules

**Audience:** Sales technicians
**Read time:** 6 minutes
**Use this when:** You are unsure whether the standard catalog safely covers the customer’s environment.

> **Sales outcome:** Know when to stop selling from assumptions and bring in technical validation.

## Require a site survey when

- Wi-Fi AP count or placement is unknown
- Existing cabling is undocumented or questionable
- Coverage includes outdoor, warehouse, kitchen, patio, or multiple floors
- The site has recurring dead zones/interference
- A new build, remodel, or relocation is underway
- Equipment racks, power, cooling, or mounting may be inadequate
- POS/printer/KDS placement is changing

## Require engineering review when

- Three or more VPN sites or complex routing
- More than 20 monitored devices at a location
- More than five APs, high density, or advanced roaming
- Multiple carriers, unusual failover, public IP/BGP requirements
- Legacy or unsupported firewalls/switches/POS
- Complex data migration or recovery objectives
- Healthcare, regulated, or contractual compliance claims
- Custom integrations, scripts, or unsupported APIs
- Customer requests guarantees, penalties, or 24/7 staffed response

## Sales technician boundary

You may gather facts, explain standard offers, and provide approved starting ranges. You may not:

- Design unreviewed complex architecture
- Guarantee compatibility
- Promise resolution times
- Approve unsupported equipment
- Commit to compliance certification
- Waive deposits, terms, or exclusions

## Escalation packet

Send engineering:

1. Customer and decision-maker
2. Business problem and impact
3. Locations and deadline
4. Device inventory and photos where authorized
5. Firewall/switch/AP/POS vendors and models
6. Carrier details
7. Cabling/site unknowns
8. Required hours/cutover window
9. Proposed Good/Better/Best direction
10. Exact questions needing approval

## Customer language

> “There are enough unknowns that I do not want to guess. The next step is a site survey and engineering review so the proposal is accurate and the implementation does not create downtime.”

Escalation is quality control—not a failure to close.
